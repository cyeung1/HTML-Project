Google Search

Introduction

Google Search, developed in 1997 by Larry Page and Sergey Brin and launched in 1998 and is currently the most popular web search on the internet. Its growth in the early 21st century has been very exponential. In 1998 it handled 10,000 search queries a day. A year later it grew it 3.5 million. In 2004 it had grown to 200 million. Nowadays in 2019 it processes 3.5 billion search queries a day and 1.2 trillion search queries a year. Out of all of the search engines Google gets 65% of the global search volume.

This popularity means that if one's website is near the top of the list in the results page the amount of exposure it gets is massive. Thus every web developer is vying for that top spot because it means a lot more traffic. To make sure they get the traffic they chase the search algorithmns that ranks search results. One such algorithmn is called PageRank.

PageRank in general assigns each web page a relevancy score. The more relevant the search term is to the web page the more it ranks the page higher in the results page.

Webpage's PageRank in general will be determined by:

  • The number and location of key words in the webpage.
    • The more the keyword appears on the page the more the higher the score will be.
  • How many webpages links to this webpage.
    • PageRank will set a webpage's score higher if there are a high number of other pages that links to it.
  • Webpage's age.
    • Webpages with an established history will get a high relevancy score.

Google's search page is the face of web search engines.

Webpage Characteristics

Google's search engine is fast and accurate. The results page displays millions of items in less than a second. The millions of results can be filtered or listed chronologically. Its design is clean and easy to navigate. Google offers other services such as image search, the popular Google Maps, News and more. Google+ and gmail softwares are developed to make google a more social experience with Twitter feeds being integrated. Google also has a mobile version for each of its services.

Google has a very robust advertisement platform but it clearly labels its ads. User's search inputs is tracked and recorded and shares information.
